Hi Carolyn,

Here is a short summary of where we landed, so you have it before the technician comes on Thursday.

The scanner reports a multifeed against pages that are single sheets and stops the batch. When you reproduced it through Kodak's own Scan Validation Tool, the same error came back as TWCC_PAPERDOUBLEFEED (0x15). That tool does not involve ScanPlus or the ISIS driver at all, so the problem sits with the scanner and not with the application. That test is what gives us a clear answer here, so thank you for running it.

One thing worth knowing: when the technician tested after the repair, multifeed sensitivity was set to None, which means ultrasonic detection was switched off for that test. It showed the paper feed and image capture were working, but it did not test the part that is actually failing.

The calibration you ran from ScanPlus did not clear the errors either, so a physical problem with the ultrasonic sensors is the more likely explanation. It could be a sensor knocked out of alignment, a loose connector, or debris in the sensor path. The technician will need to confirm which.

Two things would help on Thursday:

1. Ask him to check the ultrasonic multifeed sensor path specifically, not just the paper transport.
2. Any test after the repair should be run with ultrasonic detection on at Medium sensitivity, using your normal paper stock and a full batch.

We would like to be on the call when he visits.
